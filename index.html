Why Little Chef Academy

More Than Cooking -
A Life Skill Journey

Every class is carefully designed to nourish more than your child's appetite. We're building the next generation of confident, creative, capable humans - one recipe at a time.

🧠
Builds Real Confidence

When a child successfully creates a meal from scratch, something shifts inside them. They learn that they can try, make mistakes, adjust, and succeed - a mindset that extends far beyond the kitchen.

🥕
Develops Healthy Habits

Kids who cook are more adventurous eaters. By touching, smelling, and making food themselves, they build a natural curiosity about ingredients, nutrition, and where food comes from.

🎨
Sparks Creativity

Cooking is edible art. Our classes give children the freedom to experiment with colors, flavors, and textures - nurturing the kind of creative thinking that serves them for a lifetime.

🤝
Teaches Teamwork

In our small-group setting, children learn to share tasks, listen to each other, and celebrate each other's successes. It's community-building disguised as cooking class.

📐
Sneaky Academics

Measuring cups teach fractions. Timing teaches patience. Reading recipes builds literacy. Without ever opening a textbook, kids absorb math, science, and reading naturally.

💛
Safe, Supervised Fun

Every session is led by Autasha Smith with dedicated supervision, age-appropriate tools, and allergy-aware planning. Your child's safety and comfort are always our top priority.

Questions? We've Got Answers

Everything You Want to Know

What ages are the classes designed for?

Our core classes are designed for children ages 5–12, with activities adapted by age group. Younger children (5–7) work on foundational skills with extra support, while older kids (8–12) take on more complex techniques and independence in the kitchen.

Do kids need any cooking experience?

Absolutely not! Every class welcomes complete beginners. Whether your child has never held a spatula or already loves helping in the kitchen, there's something for every skill level. We meet each child exactly where they are.

How do you handle food allergies?

Safety first, always. We collect allergy information for every registered child before class begins. Menus are designed with common allergens in mind, and we accommodate most dietary restrictions. We'll always be upfront if a class isn't the right fit for a specific allergy.

How big are the class groups?

We cap every session at 8 children to ensure each child receives personal attention. Small groups mean more hands-on time, less waiting, and a more comfortable, encouraging experience for every little chef.

Can parents stay and watch?

This is a great question that varies by class format! Drop-off classes build independence and confidence, but we also offer parent-and-child workshops where you cook together as a team. Details will be specified for each class at registration.

What does my child take home?

The best part - everything they make! Kids go home with their creations (packed safely for travel), a printed recipe card to recreate the dish at home, and hopefully a whole lot of excitement to cook again soon.
